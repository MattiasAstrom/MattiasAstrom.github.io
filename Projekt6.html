Movement

For this project, my primary responsibility was to develop a character controller for a dungeon crawler game like Diablo.

I was responsible for creating the movement controller for a Diablo-style game. I used the MÃ¶ller-Trumbore intersection algorithm to check for collision between a raycast from the mouse and the navmesh triangles, allowing the player to move around the game world.

In addition to the movement controller, I also implemented a basic combo attack system that allowed the player to chain together attacks for more powerful hits. I also added a shield that could absorb damage and then explode, causing area of effect damage to nearby enemies.

For even more destructive power, I created a ground slam attack that would deal damage in a larger area, affecting multiple enemies at once. Finally, I implemented a ranged attack that allowed the player to hit enemies from a distance.

NavMesh

One of my main responsibility was to implement a system for loading the navmesh data from Unity into our in-house game engine using JSON. Then together with another programmer, I designed and built a data storage system that could efficiently store and retrieve the navmesh information. This data was then used to implement an A*-algorithm for pathfinding in the game. By providing this essential functionality, we enabled the other members of the team to focus on creating exciting gameplay features that could make the most of the navigation system.
